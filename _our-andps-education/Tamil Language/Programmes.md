<h4>Higher Mother Tongue Language (HMTL)</h4>
<p>A curriculum designed to stretch students who demonstrate strong aptitude
and interest in their Mother Tongue Language. It provides a more in-depth
curriculum beyond the standard MTL syllabus, incorporating complex texts,
ethnic literature, and higher-order thinking tasks to further extend and
challenge learners.</p>
<h4>Mother Tongue Support Programme (MTSP)</h4>
<p>A two-year initiative for Primary 3 and Primary 4 students, designed to
provide additional support for learners who require assistance in the learning
of their Mother Tongue Language (MTL).</p>
<h4>Mother Tongue Fortnight (MTF)</h4>
<p>Mother Tongue Fortnight is an annual two-week programme. It aims to nurture
a love for the Tamil language among students while instilling a strong
sense of pride and connection to their cultural roots.</p>
<p>Through a rich array of meaningful learning experiences beyond the classroom,
students engage in hands-on activities such as language games, quizzes,
language exchange sessions, and learning journeys. These novel and engaging
activities provide authentic opportunities for students to experience the
language in joyful and dynamic ways.</p>
<p>As students immerse themselves in these experiences, they apply the language
skills learnt in class, develop cultural literacy, and deepen their appreciation
of the beauty and richness of the Tamil language and culture.</p>

<h4>AndPS Read</h4>
<p>Under the MTL initiative, starting with Primary 1 and 2, 30 minutes of
MTL lesson per week will be dedicated to reading and reading-related activities
using the supplementary readers. Through the SOAR initiative, our youngest
Andersonians will Spark interest, Open minds, Appreciate cultural roots
and Rise as bilingual readers.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Tamil Reading Club is an ongoing programme that nurtures a love for
reading and encourages students to share their learning with peers. Through
engaging and thoughtfully curated activities, students develop critical
and creative thinking skills while deepening their interest in the Tamil
language. The club also provides opportunities for collaborative learning,
helping students broaden their perspectives and grow as confident, independent
readers.</p>
