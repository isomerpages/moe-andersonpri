<h4>National Education (NE)</h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/CCE__3.png">
</div>
<p>Through meaningful experiences and NE Commemorative Events, National Education
fosters belonging and responsibility. Students deepen their understanding
of Singapore’s identity and challenges, nurturing socially aware citizens
ready to contribute actively to their communities.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>1. Total Defence Day (TDD)<br></strong>A reminder that safeguarding
Singapore is everyone’s responsibility. Students learn the importance of
unity, resilience, and vigilance in keeping our nation safe.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>2. International Friendship Day (IFD)<br></strong>Celebrates Singapore’s
ties with the region and the world. Students appreciate diverse cultures
and understand the value of friendship and cooperation among nations.</p>
<p><strong>3. Racial Harmony Day (RHD)<br></strong>Highlights the importance
of racial and religious harmony. Students appreciate diversity, understand
different customs, and reflect on issues that shape social cohesion.</p>
<p><strong>4. National Day<br></strong>A celebration of Singapore’s resilience
and progress. Students reflect on national identity, express hopes for
the future, and strengthen their sense of pride and belonging.</p>

<h4>Peer Support and Relationships (PSR)</h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/CCE__4.png">
</div>
<p>At Anderson Primary, we believe every student has the power to make a
difference in someone else's day. Our PSR programme — anchored in the CCE
2021 Curriculum Framework — aims to build a community of care where students
are equipped and empowered to look out for one another.</p>
<p>When peer relationships are strong, students feel a deeper sense of belonging
— and that sense of connection is itself a vital safety net for those who
need help.</p>
<p>To make this a reality, our PSR programme develops students across three
interconnected domains: <strong>Peer Bonding, Peer Helping, and Peer Influencing</strong>.
Together, these nurture students who are not only well-supported, but who
actively contribute to the well-being of those around them.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 50%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/CCE_School_Environment.jpg">
</div>
<p>This commitment plays out at every level of school life through two tiers
of implementation.</p>
<p><strong>Tier 1 — Every Andersonian is a Peer Supporter</strong>
</p>
<p>Through explicit teaching of PSR knowledge and skills, all students are
equipped to care for their peers. Care Leaders in the Class Committee foster
a kind, inclusive class culture using two practical frameworks:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><em>The CHEER Model</em> guides students in supporting a peer in distress
— <em>Calm, Hear, Empathise, Encourage, Refer</em>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><em>The LOOK Model</em> cultivates a proactive mindset — actively <em>looking out</em> for
peers showing signs of distress and reaching out to <em>befriend</em> those
who may be isolated.</p>
<p></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Tier 2 — Peer Support Leaders (PSLs)</strong>
</p>
<p>These are trained student leaders who extend peer support beyond the classroom.
They strengthen our school's culture of care and lead key school-wide programmes
including the Anderson Kindness Movement, Bully-Free Campaign, and Sunbird
Wellness Club.</p>
<h4>Cyber Wellness: Empowering Digital Citizens of Tomorrow</h4>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 50%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/CCE3.png">

<p>Cyber Wellness in CCE empowers students to thrive in a digital world,
ensuring they navigate the online world with discernment and integrity.
Our curriculum is anchored by three strategic student outcomes:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Balanced Living: </strong>Embracing technology's potential while
maintaining a healthy harmony between online and offline activities.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Digital Integrity: </strong>Building a respectful and safe online
presence by using technology ethically and leaving a positive, lasting
digital footprint.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Personal Well-being: </strong>Taking personal ownership of mental
and emotional health to stay resilient and secure within the evolving digital
landscape.</p>
</li>
</ul>
