<h4>Learning Support Programme</h4>
<p>We want every child at Anderson Primary School to feel confident in maths
from day one. Our LSM programme provides gentle, early intervention for
Primary 1 to Primary 4 students who are still building their foundational
numeracy skills. Through a brief screening at the beginning of P1, we identify
students who would benefit from a more tailored approach. These students
learn during regular curriculum hours in small, focused groups, allowing
our dedicated LSM Teachers to provide the personalised attention and encouragement
they need to thrive.</p>

<h4>Spatial Visualisation Skills Programme (P1)</h4>
<p>The Spatial Visualisation Skills Programme aims to enhance our lower primary
students’ visual and spatial awareness. The use of Tangram Puzzle not only
keeps our young minds engaged but also deepens their interest and understanding
in topics which require visual-spatial skills as they move up the levels.</p>

<h4>Connecting Mathematics to the Real World</h4>
<p>We believe in bringing learning to life by showing students how mathematics
exists all around them. Through our Problem-Based Learning (PBL) approach,
students step into the shoes of mathematicians, collaborating to solve
open-ended, real-life challenges. Working together to reflect our school’s
LIGHT values, these hands-on activities foster creative problem-solving,
visualisation, and logical reasoning, proving that maths is a powerful
tool for addressing real-world challenges.</p>

<h4>Nurturing Mathematical Potential (P5 &amp; P6)</h4>
<p>We offer targeted enrichment opportunities such as the Excellence 2000
(E2K) Mathematics Programme. This inquiry-based initiative deepens conceptual
understanding and develops advanced mathematical reasoning by engaging
students in collaborative puzzles, games, and explorations. By cultivating
the inventive dispositions of true mathematicians alongside the 21st-century
competencies like critical thinking and effective communication, the programme
thoroughly prepares our learners to excel. Furthermore, students who demonstrate
exceptional aptitude are selected to represent the school in Mathematics
competition such as the Asia Pacific Mathematical Olympiad for Primary
Schools (APMOPS), the Raffles Mathematical Olympiad (RMO), and the NUS
High School National Mathematical Olympiad of Singapore (NMOS), empowering
them to continually challenge their limits and shine on a larger stage.</p>
