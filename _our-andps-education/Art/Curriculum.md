<h4>Aesthetics Assembly<br></h4>
<p>Conducted once a term to expose students to quality arts performances
and deepen appreciation. Features professional artists and curated programmes
supported by external partners, including those under the National Arts
Council (NAC)</p>
<h4>Art Inquiry Based Learning Curriculum</h4>
<p>A student-centered approach where students actively explore and investigate
topics through research and class discussion. Construct their own understanding
and meaning of artworks through experimentation with art materials individually
and collaboratively.</p>
<h4>Music Orff-Inspired Spiral Learning Curriculum</h4>
<p>Students learn music through <strong>music‑making experiences</strong> that
involve <strong>experiencing, exploring, expressing and evaluating</strong>.
Emphasis is placed on <strong>creativity, collaboration and exploration</strong>,
enabling students to participate confidently and express themselves musically
in an engaging, <strong>student‑centred learning environment</strong>. Students
are guided to appreciate music from <strong>both local and global cultures</strong>.</p>
<p><strong>Hands‑on activities</strong> such as singing with movement, rhythmic
creation, instrumental ensemble playing and song composition are <strong>purposefully designed</strong> to
encourage <strong>critical thinking and creativity</strong>. Musical concepts
are <strong>introduced, revisited and progressively built upon in a spiral manner</strong> as
students advance through the levels. <strong>Music vocabulary</strong> is
explicitly taught to empower students to <strong>communicate using musical language and critically respond to music works</strong>. <strong>21st‑century competencies</strong> are
integrated into music lessons, where students create and explore music
using <strong>digital tools</strong> such as GarageBand and Chrome Music
Lab.</p>
